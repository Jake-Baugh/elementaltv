    Prehabilitation - Medway NHS Foundation Trust

    Neil Perry, Head of Digital Transformation at Dartford & Gravesham NHS Trust (DGT) talks to Elemental TV’s Corrie Williams about the improvement of diagnostic pathways utilising Artificial Intelligence (AI) to instantly triage Chest X-Rays.

    Corrie Williams was joined by Dr. Tara Rampal, Lead Consultant Anaesthetist at Medway NHS Foundation Trust to talk about the trust’s trail-blazing Pre-habilitation programme.

    Prehabilitation is the principle of preventive, proactive medicine which has started by preparing cancer patients for major surgery using a multimodal intervention. Supervised exercise, nutrition and psychological support were the initial components of the programme, which has now expanded to a spectrum of interventions across multiple healthcare organisations offering integrated, personalised care to the patients, facing major treatment.

    Dr. Rampal discusses the patient drivers behind this initiative, the methods employed, and the successful outcomes that patients are realising as a result of this intervention.

    Harnessing Artificial Intelligence in Radiology - Dartford and Gravesham NHS Trust

    Neil Perry, Head of Digital Transformation at Dartford & Gravesham NHS Trust (DGT) talks to Elemental TV’s Corrie Williams about the improvement of diagnostic pathways utilising Artificial Intelligence (AI) to instantly triage Chest X-Rays.

    AI is being harnessed to separate radiology studies into normal and abnormal categories alerting radiographers to urgent findings at the point of acquisition and helping clinicians prioritise workloads and reduce reporting backlogs.

    The Solution has been embedded into the Clinician workflow with the Images autonomously sent to the AI platform, and the triage returned within 30 seconds back to the Radiology System.

    Results have been very positive and DGT are taking forward plans to transform pathways to benefit patients and reduce pathways for suspected Cancers by up to 2 weeks via hot reporting and introducing same day Chest CT’s.

    The Best Flow Programme - Medway NHS Foundation Trust

    Elemental TV welcomed Jack Tabner, Executive Director of Transformation, and Harvey McEnroe, Chief Operating Officer at Medway NHS Foundation Trust to discuss the trust’s transformational Best Flow programme. Hosted by Neil Stevens, Sirius Partners.

    The Best Flow programme is a large-scale transformation programme to improve patient flow through each step of the emergency access and inpatient pathways. This represents the Trust’s flagship transformation programme in 2019/20, as well as a key system priority as outlined in the trust’s local economy’s System Recovery Plan.

    The scope of the programme is broad and was shaped based on Listening Events with over 200 staff across the health economy. Improvement work has touched every aspect of the non-elective pathway, front-to-back: the Emergency Department, assessment and ambulatory areas, inpatient wards and complex discharge teams.

    The programme, run jointly with system partners Medway CCG and Medway Community Healthcare has seen some significant successes already, and is an ongoing 12-month programme of work which developing into 2020.
